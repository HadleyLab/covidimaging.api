The CIFAI is a medical research study led by Dr. Dexter Hadley at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF) to ask patients to donate their medical records for artificial intelligence research to improve cancer imaging. Dr. Hadley and the CIFAI study staff are available to answer any questions you may have.

Research studies include only people who choose to take part. Please take your time to make your decision about participating, and discuss your decision with your family or friends if you wish. If you have any questions, please feel free to ask the researchers.

Why is this study being done?

The purpose of the CIFAI study is to develop medical AI that may improve our understanding of cancer imaging which may facilitate new ways of preventing disease. To do this, we will ask you to securely donate your medical imaging and related health records to us through a secure web application. CIFAI represents the first research study that engages patients to directly donate their imaging to develop medical AI.

Am I eligible to participate in the study?

To participate, you must have had cancer imaging performed in a US institution. Your imaging may have been for screening or diagnostic purposes, and may have been either positive or negative for disease. In addition, you need to have an email account with access to a reliable internet connection or smartphone.

What will happen if I take part in the CIFAI research study?

Surveys

If you agree to participate in CIFAI, you will be asked to complete a series of surveys about your life and health using our web application. The surveys will ask about such things as your personal and demographic data, as well as medical history such as your blood pressure, cholesterol, smoking and other habits, and your history of cancer, if any. Surveys will be brief, and they will take you about 5-10 minutes to fill out. You may be asked to update your information on the CIFAI surveys every six months, or as your health changes.

Data transfer and sync

Once you agree to participate in CIFAI, we will use your survey responses to transfer all imaging and related medical data from the healthcare institutions you may have visited to our portal. We use the MatrixRay (https://matrixray.com) service by Nautilus Medical for secure transfer of imaging and related electronic health records.

Personalized dashboard

Once you agree to participate in CIFAI, you will have access to a personalized dashboard accessible through our web application portal. Here, your will be able to review all the imaging and medical records that we store for you. We will provide standardized assessments based on your cancer imaging and risk factors that you may find useful to understand your cancer risk.

How long will I be in the study?

You will remain in the study for as long as you remain a user of the web portal.

Can I stop being in the study?

Yes. You can decide to delete your account to stop being in the study. We will delete all the information about you that you have already shared, and will not contact you further.

To stop participating in the study, please send an email to the study staff at: dexter.hadley@ucsf.edu or call Dexter Hadley at 415-514-0513.

What side effects or risks can I expect from being in the study?

Although we will do our best to protect your study information (see below), there is still a very small risk of loss of privacy. There is a risk that participating in the study will make you want to access the healthcare system more often, which costs money. For example, we may provide assessments of your cancer imaging on your personalized dashboard, such as cancer risk estimates, directly to you, which might prompt you to want to talk to your health care provider. Whereas there may be benefits to you from seeing your healthcare provider, there are also costs, and the CIFAI will not pay for any of your medical care. You should not see your healthcare provider solely for the purpose of collecting data for the CIFAI.

For more information about risks and side effects, please ask one of the researchers. You can reach Dr. Hadley at dexter.hadley@ucsf.edu. If you want to speak to someone not directly involved in the research study, please contact the UCSF Institutional Review Board at 415-476-1814.

Are there benefits to taking part in the study?

You will not obtain any direct health benefits from participating in the study. You may directly benefit from having access to your medical information across institutions in a single database that you may share with your healthcare provider either in person or through the portal. We hope that society will benefit from your participation – by participating, you will help us contribute to a better understanding of cancer and we may find better ways to predict, prevent, and treat the disease.

Will I get advice about my health from the study?

No. We will not provide you with information about your disease or clinical interpretation of your data from the study. CIFAI is a research study and is not clinical care. We do not provide medical services. However, you will receive standardized information about cancer risk factors based on the imaging and surveys you provide through a personalized dashboard. Specifically, we will provide the Breast Cancer Surveillance Consortium Risk Calculator 5-year estimates of breast cancer based on your imaging and demographic information you share. Participation in the CIFAI does not in any way substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment that your doctor or other healthcare provider may give you. Always ask the advice of your healthcare provider if you have any questions about a medical condition. Do not disregard professional medical advice or delay in seeking care because of something you have read as part of the CIFAI study. If you think you may have a medical emergency, please call your doctor or dial 911 immediately.

What other choices do I have if I do not take part in the CIFAI study?

You are free to choose not to participate in the study. If you decide not to take part in this study, there will be no penalty to you.

How is my information protected?

We will do our best to make sure that the personal information we collect about you is kept private and secure. The CIFAI will never sell, rent, or lease your personal or contact information without your consent. If we do make your information available to other research groups, we will inform you beforehand how we will protect your privacy, and you will be able to opt out from any use of your cancer imaging at that time. If information from the CIFAI study is published or presented at scientific meetings or shared with other researchers, your name and other personal identifiers will never be used. However, we cannot guarantee total privacy – your personal information may be given out if required by law (e.g., to prevent possible injury to yourself or others).






Your information will be transmitted and stored using very secure systems within government-regulated HIPAA privacy guidelines. We label your images and information only with a code, and we keep the key to the code in a password protected database. Only approved staff will be given the password. We use other safeguards at our facilities and for our information technology and systems to protect the privacy and security of your information.

What are the costs of taking part in the CIFAI study?

You will not be charged to take part in the study. Some of the study components may result in the use of some cellular data.

Will I be paid for taking part in the CIFAI study?

You will not be paid for taking part in the CIFAI study.

What are my rights if I take part in the CIFAI study?

Taking part in the CIFAI study is your choice. You may choose either to take part or not to take part in the study. If you decide to take part in this study, you may leave the study at any time. No matter what decision you make, there will be no penalty to you.

By consenting to be in this study you do not waive any of your legal rights. Consenting means that you have been given information about this study and that you agree to participate in the study. You will be given a copy of this form to keep.

If you do not agree to be in this study or if at any time you withdraw from this study you will not suffer any penalty or lose any benefits to which you are entitled. Your participation is completely up to you. Your decision will not affect your ability to get health care or payment for your health care. It will not affect your enrollment in any health plan or benefits you can get.

We will tell you about new information or changes in the study that may affect your willingness to continue in the study.

Who can answer my questions about the study?

You can contact Dr. Hadley at dexter.hadley@ucsf.edu.

You may also call 415-476-1814 or email irb@ucsf.edu to reach the UCSF Institutional Review Board (IRB). The IRB is a group that helps monitor research. You should call or email the IRB if you want to find out about your rights as a research subject. You should also call or email if you want to talk to someone who is not part of the study about your questions, concerns, or problems.
